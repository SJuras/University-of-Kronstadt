Lucius Kronstadt

Our University was founded by professor Lucius Kronstadt in 1973.
A renowned mathematician and physicist with a keen interest for propulsion, orbital mechanics and space exploration.

Professor Kronstadt had a vision, a vision of new generations of space-program scientists and engineers from all over the world, working together for the benefit of humanity.
Science knows no borders, just as human ingenuity knows no boundaries. It is our destiny to reach the stars, together as one.

Professor's vision came true. With every new generation of graduates in fields of STEM emerging from our University, we are one step closer to fulfilling humanity's dream of stars.

Project Agamemnon

In 2019 our graduated students have taken part in a joint project to design, build and test a reusable rocket design, capable of performing multiple missions in LEO (Lower Earth Orbit) and returning safely back on the ground using its own engines to manoeuvre the safe landing.

Project Agamemnon was born, as a joint venture between NASA, USAF and scientists and engeneers from the University of Kronstadt.

After only one year, the project was ready for it's first test flight.

First preliminary trials and tests showed promissing results. Few setbacks were present but were eventually solved.

On the 10th of May 2020, Agamemnon took to the skies and than to the stars on it's first mission to LEO, delivering a small satellite for USAF. After deployment, the lower booster part of the Agamemnon safely landed at the initial launching site.

This marked the first successful flight of the Kronstadt's space project.
Agamemnon's success paved way to next phase of reusable rocket projects - project Hector.
